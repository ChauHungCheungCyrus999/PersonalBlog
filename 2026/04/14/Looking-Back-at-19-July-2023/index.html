Looking Back at 19 July 2023 - Result Day of Dse

It’s been two and a half years since that day. Some of the details are starting to blur, so I wanted to write down the parts I still remember vividly before they disappear.

The Last Assembly

It was a typical sunny Hong Kong morning. All of us were called into the main hall of LPSMC. I remember looking around and realizing this was likely the last time our entire year—Classes A through E—would ever sit together. The principal and teachers were making small talk, eventually moving on to announce the high achievers and those who scored 5s or above.

Obviously, I wasn’t on that list. 😂😂😂

After the assembly, we were sent to our classrooms. My class, 6E, was assigned to the room closest to the main gate. As we walked down, I turned to Tommy and said:

“GNG, I am pretty cooked, ngl.”

I don’t even remember what he replied; I just remember that heavy, sinking feeling in my gut.

The Moment of Truth

Sitting in the back right corner of the classroom, the atmosphere was thick with nerves. I looked around at the familiar desks and thought, “This is it. I don’t think I’m getting into uni. What kind of job am I even going to do?”

Miss Lam, our class teacher, stood at the front. “Alright, let’s distribute the results,” she said. “This is the last time I’ll ever hand anything out to you guys. Let’s start with Student 1.”

That was me. Student #1.

I walked up, surprisingly numb because I had already accepted defeat. When she handed me the paper, I couldn’t bring myself to look. Miss Lam whispered, “I know you can.” In my head, I was just thinking, “Bruh, what are you even talking about…?”

I walked back to my seat with the paper face down. My heart was racing (and okay, maybe I was sweating a little). My friends immediately swarmed me. “GNG, I haven’t looked yet!” I told them. It felt like opening a pack of Pokémon cards—hoping for a rare pull but fully expecting a common.

Tommy said, “Let me look for you.” I handed it over. He looked at it with a total poker face—no smile, no emotion. I was panicking: “Bro, what is that face?” After a few seconds, he just said, “Not bad, not bad,” and passed it back.

The Score

I waited until Miss Lam finished calling the last student before I finally flipped it over. My eyes blurred across the page. Why are there so many 3s and 4s? I started counting my Best 5.

“DAMN, I GOT 19?!”

My last mock exam predicted a 12. I was in total shock. My LS teacher came over and asked for my grade; when she saw the paper, she looked just as surprised as I was.

Pranking My Mum

I needed air, so I headed out to the canteen to call my mum. I saw a missed call from her already. When I called back, she picked up on the first ring, her voice tight with anxiety.

“Son, how is it?”

I couldn’t help myself; I had to prank her.
“It’s… not really good,” I said, sounding disappointed.
“How many marks?” she asked, sounding devastated.
“9,” I said.
“What?! 9?!”
“19, actually.”
“WOW, 19! That means you can get into university!”

I sat on the canteen benches for a while with Jimmy and Leo. They hadn’t gotten the grades they wanted, and I remember Leo looking pretty down while Jimmy was just chilling. (Looking at their IG now, they’re doing great—Jimmy’s gotten fit and Leo is always traveling with his girlfriend. Life works out!)

I packed my bag and left my secondary school for the last time. GG, LPSMC.

When I got home, I bumped into my mum at the 13/F lift lobby. She gave me a huge hug, even though I was drenched in sweat from the July heat.

Final Thoughts

I am putting this into words because I refuse to let this memory disappear. I want to look back years from now and remember the kid who was waiting for that envelope and, hopefully, have a good laugh about it all.

Tbh, the DSE is just a small part of life, but it was an unforgettable lesson.
